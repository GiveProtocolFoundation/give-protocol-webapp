# First Steps

You've created your account and verified your email. Here's what to do next.

## For Donors

### 1. Complete Your Profile

Head to **Profile Settings** (`/settings`) and fill in your display name, preferred currency, and notification preferences. See [Setting Up Your Profile](./setting-up-profile.md) for a full walkthrough.

### 2. Connect a Wallet

If you plan to donate cryptocurrency, connect your wallet now so it's ready when you find a cause you love. Click **Connect Wallet** in the navigation bar and follow the prompts.

Supported wallets include MetaMask, WalletConnect, Coinbase Wallet, Ledger, and more. See [Personal Wallet Setup](./personal-wallet-setup.md) for the full list.

> **Prefer fiat?** No wallet needed. Look for **Pay with Card** on the donation form to donate via credit or debit card.

### 3. Browse Charities

Go to `/browse` to explore verified 501(c)(3) nonprofits. You can:

- Search by name or keyword
- Filter by cause category (education, housing, environment, etc.)
- Sort by most recent or most active

Click any charity card to view its full profile, causes, and donation options.

### 4. Make Your First Donation

On any charity's detail page, click **Donate**.

1. Choose your donation type: **Crypto** or **Fiat (Card)**
2. Select the token and network (for crypto) or enter your card details (for fiat)
3. Enter the donation amount
4. Review the transaction summary — note the 0.5% platform fee
5. Click **Confirm Donation** and approve in your wallet (for crypto)

After the transaction is confirmed on-chain, you'll receive a donation receipt by email.

### 5. Explore Charitable Equity Funds (CEFs)

Give Protocol offers **Charitable Equity Funds (CEFs)** — pooled giving vehicles that distribute your donation across multiple charities aligned with a cause. Browse available funds from your Donor Dashboard (`/give-dashboard`) or navigate directly to individual fund pages such as `/portfolio/environmental`, `/portfolio/education`, or `/portfolio/poverty`.

### 6. Set Up Recurring Donations

For ongoing impact, set up automatic monthly or quarterly donations at `/scheduled-donations`. Select a charity, amount, frequency, and token. The smart contract will process each payment automatically on schedule.

## For Charities

### 1. Set Up Your Treasury Wallet

Before you can receive donations, you need to register a wallet address in your Charity Portal. Give Protocol recommends a **Safe multisig wallet** for all organizations — it requires multiple signers to approve any transaction, which protects your funds and meets standard nonprofit governance requirements.

See [Organizational Treasury Wallet Setup](./organizational-wallet-setup.md) for step-by-step instructions covering Safe multisig, institutional custody, and (for very small orgs only) single-signer options.

### 2. Complete Your Organization Profile

After your account is verified, go to the **Charity Portal** (`/charity-portal`) and fill in:

- Organization description and mission statement
- Logo and cover image
- Website and contact email
- Donation wallet address

See [Setting Up Your Profile](./setting-up-profile.md) for details.

### 3. Add Causes

Create one or more **Causes** in your portal — these are specific programs or initiatives donors can direct their giving to. Each cause has its own title, description, and funding goal.

### 4. Post Volunteer Opportunities

Use the Volunteer section of the Charity Portal to list open volunteer positions. Describe the role, required skills, and schedule. Volunteers who apply and complete hours will be verified on-chain.

### 5. Share Your Profile

Once your profile is live, share your Give Protocol page with your donor community. Your public URL is `giveprotocol.io/charity/{your-slug}`.

## For Volunteers

### 1. Browse Opportunities

Go to `/opportunities` to see all open volunteer positions. Filter by cause, skill, location, or availability.

### 2. Apply for a Position

Click any opportunity card to view details and apply. The charity will review your application and reach out.

### 3. Log Your Hours

After completing volunteer work, log your hours through the platform. The charity administrator will verify and record your contribution on-chain.

### 4. View Your Service Record

Track your verified hours at `/contributions`. Your contribution history is stored on-chain and portable — you can share it with future employers or organizations.
